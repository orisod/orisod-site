What is a HEIC file?

HEIC is the image format used by default on iPhones and iPads since iOS 11. It keeps high image quality while using roughly half the storage space of an equivalent JPG. The tradeoff is compatibility — many Windows computers, websites, and apps outside the Apple ecosystem can't open HEIC files directly, which is why converting to JPG is often necessary.

When should you use this tool?

  • You transferred iPhone photos to a Windows PC and they won't open
  • A website or form rejects your photo because it doesn't support HEIC
  • You want to share a photo with someone whose device can't display HEIC
  • You need to edit the photo in software that only accepts JPG or PNG

Frequently asked questions

Why won't my iPhone photos open on my computer?
Windows and many older programs don't natively support the HEIC format. Converting to JPG makes the photo viewable and editable everywhere.
Will I lose photo quality converting to JPG?
At quality settings above 80%, the difference is generally not noticeable. You can adjust the quality slider to balance size and sharpness.
Are my photos uploaded to a server?
No. The decoding and conversion both happen locally in your browser — your photos never leave your device.
Does this also work with HEIF files?
Yes, HEIF (the general container format) and HEIC (Apple's specific variant) are both supported.
