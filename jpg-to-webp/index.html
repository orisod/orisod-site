What is JPG to WEBP conversion?

WEBP is a modern image format developed by Google that typically produces 25–35% smaller files than JPG at the same visual quality. Converting your JPG photos to WEBP means faster page loads if you're using them on a website, and less storage space if you're archiving large photo collections.

When should you use this tool?

  • You're uploading images to a website and want faster load times
  • You need to reduce storage space for a large batch of photos
  • Your website platform (Shopify, WordPress, etc.) recommends or requires WEBP images
  • You want smaller email attachments without losing visible quality

Frequently asked questions

Will converting to WEBP reduce image quality?
At quality settings above 75%, most people can't tell the difference visually, even though the file is significantly smaller. You control the quality slider, so you can balance size versus sharpness.
Do my images get uploaded anywhere?
No. The conversion happens locally in your browser using JavaScript — your files never leave your device or touch a server.
Is there a limit to how many files I can convert?
No daily limit. You can select and convert multiple JPG files at once, and download them individually or all together.
Do all browsers and websites support WEBP?
Yes, WEBP is supported by all modern browsers (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge) and is widely accepted by content management systems and image hosts.
